Thanks for checking out my website!

I'm Toyo and i'm interested in all kinds of tech, game developement and art.
Over the years i've gained a lot of knowledge about the general concepts of programming, largely through a game series called "LittleBigPlanet".

LittleBigPlanet introduced me to the concept of programming and game developement at an early age, over the years i mastered it until i was pretty much able to programm anything in it. It's shocking how powerful the visual programming language of that series is, i wished it would've been on pc and allowed you to export your games but sadly everything that you create in it is locked to inside of the game.

In the meantime they've also released another engine-game called "Dreams" but at this point i wasn't dedicated to fully learning that anymore, it also has the exact same problems as its predecessor.

So now i'm starting to learn "real" game developement engines and im going to document my progress and everything between inside of my blogs in the hopes that it may help somebody else and also for myself. In addition to that i am also going to showcase my already existing game projects on here and provide some insights into their creation processes.

At this point i am also running a custom server for the aforementioned game series as the official servers have (unsurprisingly) been shut down. I'm using open source software for this, which is convenient for me as i'm actually not very experienced in networking. We also have a relatively big team at this point alongside a Discord Server, it's my most successful project by far so there will be a seperate page dedicated to it.

I also have an (inactive) YouTube channel and a lot more social media, almost all of which you'll be able to find on my social media page. I'm also learning html and css right now so this website will definitely improve in functionality and design as time goes on and i gain more experience. Oh, and im also bilingual, i speak both german and english fluently and see both as my "native" languages, since i've grown up learning both. I use English primarily though, for pretty much everything that doesn't involve speaking with people in real life (including thinking most of the time) as i live in Germany.

Anyway, i hope that's enough of an introduction and that you'll enjoy following my journey!
